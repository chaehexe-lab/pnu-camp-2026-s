# Output Format

Adapt sections to the user's request while preserving evidence traceability.

## 1. Analysis conditions

List exam scope, materials actually analyzed, unavailable sources, assumptions, and confidence limits.

## 2. Professor emphasis map

| Rank | Concept | Weight | Evidence and location | Confidence |
|---:|---|---:|---|---|

Keep evidence descriptions concise and location-specific.

## 3. Student–professor alignment

| Student priority | Classification | Matching professor concept | Feedback |
|---|---|---|---|

Then report weighted coverage, missed high-priority concepts, and a short study recommendation. Explain how partial matches were counted.

## 4. Exam blueprint

| Concept | Learning demand | Format | Difficulty | Items | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---:|---|

## 5. Practice exam

Provide title, suggested time, instructions, point values, and numbered questions. Omit analysis/source annotations here when the user wants a clean test sheet.

## 6. Answer key and explanations

For each item provide:

- answer;
- concise reasoning;
- source location;
- scoring criteria or distractor analysis when applicable;
- concept and difficulty tag.

## 7. Final coverage check

Summarize coverage by emphasis tier and disclose any weakly supported or provisional questions.
